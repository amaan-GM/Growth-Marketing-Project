## Post 2: Company LinkedIn page strategy

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/devinreed_still-ignoring-your-company-linkedin-page-activity-7467646922816811008-Mx5L/

Still ignoring your company LinkedIn page? New data reveals why that could be preventing you from winning deals. 

Dreamdata analyzed 3.5 million customer journeys to discover LinkedIn’s impact on B2B marketing. 

They found Company Page impressions appear in:

9.4% of MQLs
15% of SQLs1
7.9% of New Business Deals 

Clearly skimming LinkedIn company pages is part of the eval process. 
So we have to use it as a persuasive tool, not an afterthought. 

Some must-haves:

1. Clear and matching value props.
-Make sure it matches your website (they’re heading there next). Don’t get clever.
-Outdated messaging confuses would-be buyers


2. Tell your product story
-Thought leadership content is best served from your employees, execs and founder.
-Use this real estate to tell your product announcements, use cases, and corp events. 


3. Complete every single field and section
-Sounds obvious. It isn't.
-LLMs love LinkedIn (#2 cited source right now)
-Don’t lose out on visibility because your profile is incomplete. 


Imagine your ideal prospect finds your page - What will they see?

And most importantly, is it showing how you’re the right choice?

There are more insights from Dreamdata’s LinkedIn Ads Benchmark Report 2026.

Each one will reshape how you use LinkedIn to grow your business.

## Post 3: B2B marketer request overload

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/devinreed_if-youre-a-b2b-marketer-drowning-in-requests-activity-7455315156588318720-ZJ3R/

If you're a B2B marketer drowning in requests and wondering what to prioritize — read this.

I've led and advised dozens of B2B content teams and the hardest part is never the content itself. 

It's knowing where to start when everything feels urgent.

My career changed once I figured out how to handle the overwhelm. 

Here's what's helped me:

𝟭. 𝗜𝗳 𝘆𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝗖𝗘𝗢 𝗸𝗲𝗲𝗽𝘀 𝗮𝘀𝗸𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘆𝗼𝘂 𝗳𝗼𝗿 "𝗼𝗻𝗲 𝗺𝗼𝗿𝗲 𝘁𝗵𝗶𝗻𝗴" 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝘆𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝘀𝘁𝗿𝗮𝘁𝗲𝗴𝘆 𝗶𝘀 𝗻𝗼𝘄 𝗮 𝘄𝗶𝘀𝗵𝗹𝗶𝘀𝘁...
→ Start with CEO goals, not your content calendar

The first question isn't "what should we create?" It's "what are our top three company priorities — and how does content impact them?" Everything else flows from there. This makes your job easier and your CEO happier.

𝟮. 𝗜𝗳 𝘆𝗼𝘂'𝗿𝗲 𝗯𝗲𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗮𝘀𝗸𝗲𝗱 𝘁𝗼 𝗰𝗿𝗲𝗮𝘁𝗲 𝗠𝗢𝗥𝗘 𝗰𝗼𝗻𝘁𝗲𝗻𝘁 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝘄𝗼𝗻𝗱𝗲𝗿𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗵𝗼𝘄 𝘁𝗳 𝗮𝗺 𝗜 𝗴𝗼𝗻𝗻𝗮 𝗱𝗼 𝘁𝗵𝗮𝘁...
→ Repurpose like hell and build a content waterfall

This is the fastest way to win. One great piece of content — webinar, podcast, report — can fuel LinkedIn posts, blogs, and email all at once. Starting from scratch should be your last resort.


𝟯. 𝗜𝗳 𝘆𝗼𝘂'𝗿𝗲 𝗱𝗼𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘁𝗼𝗼 𝗱𝗮𝗺𝗻 𝗺𝘂𝗰𝗵 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝗱𝗼𝗻’𝘁 𝗸𝗻𝗼𝘄 𝘄𝗵𝗮𝘁’𝘀 𝘄𝗼𝗿𝘁𝗵 𝗸𝗲𝗲𝗽𝗶𝗻𝗴...
→ Measure growth, not just outputs

Pull the data on what's working. If something isn't driving pipeline, audience growth, or brand — kill it or replace it with something better. You could probably stop half your content today and no one would notice. That's not a failure, that's being lean. 


I spent years drowning in the same requests. Turns out the answer was never more content — it was knowing exactly what to say no to.
