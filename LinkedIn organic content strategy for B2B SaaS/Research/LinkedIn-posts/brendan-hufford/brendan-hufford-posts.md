## Post 1: AI and content work

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/brendanhufford_after-100s-of-hours-with-ai-im-realizing-activity-7470109940729229312-ESGG/

After 100s of hours with AI, I'm realizing Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity... they're all worthless until you do these 4 things:

You know the ability to pick between three headlines and know which one will land?

How to sense when your marketing is technically correct but nobody's going to care?

It's called TASTE.

Good news: taste is something you can learn (and get good at).

Sam Parr described it recently and I put it into a framework so I can remember it.

I call it the 4D framework and it's honestly the answer to the whole "AI is coming for your job" panic.

1. Decide what you actually want to say.
2. Duplicate the people already saying it well. Blindly. Shamelessly.
3. Decode the rules underneath why it works.
4. Defy the rules

Whether you’re studying Apple or Dr. Dre, it’s the same pattern.

People decided what they wanted to say, studied what was already out there, figured out why it worked and then decided what rules to break.

AI can match patterns. It can't decide which pattern to break. 

That's where YOU live, my friend. That's where the value lives.

If you're spending all your time building workflows to remove yourself from the process, you might be automating the wrong part.

And when everyone's letting AI do the thinking, we’re gonna be the ones making stuff worth caring about.

PS - I could automate my client "3S" interviews. I like doing them. I catch things. Ideas hit me mid-conversation. Not everything that can be automated should be.

## Post 3: Customer journey content

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/brendanhufford_the-customer-journey-youre-writing-content-activity-7462864814470647809-rDj2/

The “customer journey” you’re writing content for? 

Try 10 stakeholders, 88 touches, and 326 days to revenue.

Compared to last year?

+3 more stakeholders
+12 more touchpoints
+44 days to revenue for SMB

I’ve lost count of how many times I’ve seen execs describe the marketing “funnel” as if it’s neat, clean, and linear: one person, one problem, one path to purchase.

But Dreamdata data shows a different reality:

- 10 decision-makers are involved in the average B2B deal.
- There are 88 touches across 4 different channels.
- It can take 326 days from first impression to revenue for larger organizations.

Here’s what that means for us on the organic/content side:

1. You’re not writing for a single “persona.” You’re writing for a buying committee.
2. You can’t expect one ebook or webinar to move the needle.
3. You can’t judge success in weeks. 

AKA - STOP TRYING TO SHOW ROI IN QUARTER.

Real influence builds over months (sometimes a year).

Real talk: most of us are about to start Q4.

This means gearing up for some serious reporting.

You need to get this data in front of your leadership team BEFORE then.

Because even if you’re doing the best work of your life… the illusion that’s been sold to your board and exec team that buyers move through it in a straight line can still derail your work.

If you want to build your content strategy for reality, it has to be focused on the messy middle.

Calling it a funnel is polite. In reality, it’s a 88-touch pinball machine.
