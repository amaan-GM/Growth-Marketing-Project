## Post 1: Claude Code workflow for content

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/diandraescobar_i-just-built-a-claude-code-workflow-that-activity-7471556429766701056-j8Rz/

"I just built a Claude Code workflow that helps me create 3,075 pieces of content in less than five minutes every week." ... yes Karen, we can tell.

Everyone wants to find the magical prompt to get AI to write their posts perfectly. 

Almost nobody has a space where they actually think.

If you've never forced yourself to think a topic all the way through, you have nothing for AI to work with. So you ask it to generate from scratch and you get the slop everyone complains about.

A LinkedIn post is too tight for real thinking. A tweet is too short. You can't dig into your own gaps in 200 characters.

Or why a single good post takes you hours, and why you can't wrap your head around how anyone posts seven times a week.

Long form feeds short form. 

Don't run it the other way.

That's why I record every client call. Sit with my team on their strategy. Why I write my newsletter. Why I sit with a 20 minute YouTube video before it ever becomes eight posts. Build my own lead magnets.

For the simple reason that:

I NEED A FREAKIN SPACE TO ACTUALLY THINK.

I have an agency. I could outsource all of it to people better than me. 

I don't, because HOW WOULD I BE A CONTENT EXPERT IF I DON'T THINK ABOUT CONTENT?

Those are my deep spaces. somewhere I'm forced to be messy, criticize my own ideas, and find where my thinking falls apart.

You can't run it the other way. A choppy little AI whipped up post can't be repurposed into anything deeper, because there was never anything deeper behind it.

Most people skip this and wonder why they run out of things to say by week three. They never gave themselves room to go deep on anything.

Pick your deep space. a video, newsletter, a podcast, your calls. Protect the time for it.

Then AI actually has something to do. 

And so do you.

## Post 2: LinkedIn Steps you can't skip

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/diandraescobar_we-were-sold-the-idea-that-posting-is-the-activity-7470457277410607105-HxIa/

We were sold the idea that posting is the only thing standing between us and winning on LinkedIn. But posting is just ONE link in a chain. 

And chains don’t break at their strongest point. 

They break at the weakest one.

Winning on LinkedIn has been sold to us as a big number. A viral post, a follower count, a screenshot of impressions.

Real winning is quieter. It's the right person reading you for weeks, never liking a thing, then showing up in your DMs ready to buy.

That only happens when they trust you.

And trust isn't one post, it's the same person seeing you be useful, over and over, until you're the obvious choice.

Every step below exists to build that trust and turn it into pipeline. Miss one and the chain breaks somewhere you won't notice until the leads don't come.

Here are the nine, and what each one actually gets you:

1. A defined ICP → a message that lands

Scream their pain point back at them, then drip solutions through your content. If you define who it's for, the words get sharper on their own.

2. A profile built for search and algorithm → profile traffic

Your profile is the conversion point. Every post is the ad that sends people there, so it has to sell when they arrive and it has to help the algorithm put you there.

3. Daily ICP comments → an audience

Posting to zero followers will burn you out, trust me. Comments are how you get in front of other people's audiences while building your own at the same time.

4. Consistency → compounding

One good post is luck. Consistency is what teaches people who you are. The more often the right people see you, the faster trust compounds.

5. Value or curiosity → a "see more"

The first lines have one job: earn the click. Give people a reason to stay curious, promise value, or open a gap they need closed.

6. A "how I" frame → trust

"How to" is advice anyone can give. "How I" is lived experience. The more specific your experience is, the harder it becomes to copy.

7. Saves and sends → a real signal

Likes are passive. Saves and sends tell the algorithm someone cared enough to come back to it or share it with someone else.

8. Conversion posts → DMs

At some point you have to ask for the business. Built on the first seven, asking feels natural instead of desperate.

9. A lead magnet → an email list

Attention you don't capture is attention you rent. A lead magnet turns a reader into someone you can reach again.

Most people want to start at step five, the viral hook. 

The first four are boring, which is why almost nobody does them.

Get the foundations right, and everything after that gets easier.

## Post 3: Client impressions and content system

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/diandraescobar_a-client-of-ours-pulled-824k-impressions-activity-7468279103163596800-WOb5/

A client of ours pulled 824k impressions from 4 LinkedIn posts in 3 months. There's a reason I won't shut up about the 4 buckets, and this is it:

Those 4 posts were Growth posts.

Their ONLY job was to bring new eyes in. Bold openers that picked a fight with the consensus or cracked open a curiosity loop wide enough that you had to keep reading.

But Growth posts on their own don't close deals. They open the door.

Once people landed on his profile, the Authority posts did the heavy lifting. Frameworks, breakdowns, case studies, the actual thinking behind the hot takes.

That's where trust got built.

This is the part the "post viral takes" advice leaves out. A viral hook gets you eyeballs. The system behind it gets you the contract.

Growth gets attention. Authority earns the meeting. Conversion turns it into pipeline.

Growth (40%) brings the eyes.
Authority (30%) earns the trust.
Conversion (20%) drives the action.
Personal (10%) makes them remember you.

Skip Growth and you're invisible. Skip Authority and you're noisy. Skip Conversion and you're a free media company. Skip Personal and they forget your name.

If you've followed me for a while, you know I won't shut up about the 4 buckets. There's a reason. I've watched this exact system turn invisible founders into 6-7 figure businesses.

His 824k impressions made for a nice screenshot. The system behind them is what landed the contract.

...This post is an Authority post. 

It won't pull numbers like his Growth posts did, and it doesn't need to. It's doing for me what his Authority posts did for him: building trust with the people already here.

This is the system we run for every Distinctiva client.
