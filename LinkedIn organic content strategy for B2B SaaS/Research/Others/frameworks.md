# Key LinkedIn Frameworks Identified

## 1. Adam Robinson: Edutainment Framework
50% teaching + 50% storytelling in every post.
Write to your former self, not to impress, but to help the person you used to be.

## 2. Devin Reed: 95-5 Content System
95% of your buyers are not ready to buy right now.
Create content for them first. When they are ready, you are already the only name they trust.

## 3. Peep Laja: Data-Backed Contrarian Method
Never post opinions without evidence.
Use proprietary research and buyer survey data to make bold claims that others are afraid to make.

## 4. Brendan Hufford: 3S Strategy
Mine Sales, Success, and Support conversations for LinkedIn content ideas. 
The best posts come from real questions real buyers are already asking.

## 5. Diandra Escobar: Organic Content Flywheel
Turn founder expertise into a content engine.
LinkedIn is the trust layer. Everything else (YouTube, newsletter, DMs) feeds from that trust.

## 6. Anthony Pierri: Positioning-First Content
Clarity before content. If your positioning is unclear, no amount of LinkedIn posts will fix it.
Start with who the product is for and why it wins.

## 7. Gal Aga: Hook Bank System
Build a library of 100+ proven hooks organised by category. 
Never start a post from a blank page.
Pair every hook with a documented weekly writing workflow.

## 8. Dave Gerhardt: Brand-Led Growth
Brand is not a logo. Brand is what people say about you when you are not in the room.
Build community first, pipeline follows.

## 9. Amanda Natividad: Zero Click Marketing
Deliver the full value inside the post itself.
Never hide the best insight behind a link click.
The feed is the product.

## 10. Chris Walker: Demand Creation vs Lead Gen
Stop chasing leads & start creating demand.
Dark social is where real B2B buying decisions happen, before anyone fills out a form.
